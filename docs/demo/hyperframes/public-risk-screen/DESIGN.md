# Orbital Inspect Public Risk Screen Demo - Visual Identity

## Style Prompt
Premium orbital operations film: a restrained dark command-room canvas, sharp evidence typography, glass instrument panels, cinematic light sweeps, and precise motion that feels like a mission review rather than a marketing splash. The visual system should communicate public-data humility, auditability, and high-assurance product craft.

## Colors
- Deep space background: `#070B12`
- Instrument panel: `#101824`
- Porcelain text: `#EDF4F8`
- Muted slate text: `#9BB0BE`
- Verification green: `#7CF2B7`
- Caution amber: `#F1B55C`
- Signal blue: `#75A7FF`

## Typography
- Display voice: `Fraunces`, 900 weight, tight but readable.
- Data voice: `IBM Plex Mono`, 400-700 weight, tabular numerals.

## Motion Rules
- Use focus-pull reveals, fast data wipes, and orbiting linework.
- Transitions should reveal the next scene over the current one; no jump cuts.
- Data labels animate like instrumentation coming online, not like generic cards.
- Ambient motion remains slow and deterministic.

## What NOT To Do
- Do not imply NASA/SpaceX certification.
- Do not claim public data produces final underwriting decisions.
- Do not use purple-blue gradient hero styling.
- Do not use generic neon HUD clutter or unreadable microtext.
- Do not make the product screenshots secondary to decoration.
