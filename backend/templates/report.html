APPENDIX A — METHODOLOGY {{ report_id }}
Appendix A  —  Methodology
A.1  Assessment Pipeline

This report is produced by the Orbital Inspect multi-agent analysis pipeline. Each stage is executed by a specialized AI agent and its outputs are forwarded as structured context to downstream stages. The pipeline is designed to replicate the analytical reasoning of a senior satellite insurance engineer.

1
CLASSIFICATION AGENT
Identifies satellite type, bus platform, orbital regime, design life, and expected component configuration from imagery and metadata. Draws on a curated database of over 800 known satellite designs.
2
VISION ANALYSIS AGENT
Performs computer vision analysis of the primary satellite image to identify structural anomalies, surface damage, solar array degradation, antenna deformation, and thermal coating deterioration. Returns bounding boxes, severity classification, confidence scores, and estimated power impact per anomaly.
3
ENVIRONMENT ASSESSMENT AGENT
Queries NASA ORDEM debris flux model, NOAA SWPC solar activity data, CelesTrak orbital conjunction analysis, and AP-8/AE-8 radiation belt models to characterize the operational environment at the satellite's orbital regime.
4
FAILURE MODE ANALYSIS AGENT
Correlates visual damage patterns with known failure modes using a knowledge base of historical satellite anomaly and total loss events. Constructs a root cause chain and assesses progression rate drawing on NASA GSFC, ESA, and Lloyd's claims data.
5
INSURANCE RISK AGENT
Synthesizes all upstream agent outputs to produce the three-axis risk matrix (Severity × Probability × Consequence), composite score, financial exposure model, underwriting recommendation, and recommended actions. Follows Lloyd's Space Market Association underwriting guidelines.
6
REPORT GENERATOR
Renders this document from structured agent outputs. Charts are generated server-side using matplotlib. Damage overlays are rendered using Pillow image processing. All assets are embedded as base64 data URIs for a self-contained, offline-capable document.

A.3  Confidence Framework

Each agent assigns a confidence score (0.0–1.0) to its primary outputs based on image quality, data completeness, and precedent coverage. Scores below 0.60 trigger evidence gap flags and degrade the overall report completeness status. Composite report confidence is the harmonic mean of all agent confidence scores.

  • This assessment is based on a single-epoch image. Temporal change detection requires multiple imagery epochs and is outside scope unless explicitly provided.
  • Internal component damage (propellant tank integrity, electrical harness, battery cell health) cannot be assessed from external imagery and is not covered by this report.

  • Orbital conjunction data is accurate as of report generation date. Conjunction risk is dynamic and should be re-evaluated at each insurance renewal.
  • Historical precedent claims data is drawn from publicly reported events. Private settlements are not represented and actual market loss experience may differ.
  • All financial exposure estimates are indicative only. Actual replacement cost and revenue figures must be confirmed with the operator and broker before policy issuance.

APPENDIX C — GLOSSARY {{ report_id }}
Appendix C  —  Glossary
C.1  Insurance Terms
Composite Risk Score
A single integer (0–125) derived as Severity × Probability × Consequence (each scored 1–5). Used as the primary quantitative risk signal for underwriting decisions.
Underwriting Recommendation
The formal Orbital Inspect recommendation to the underwriter. Categories: INSURABLE_STANDARD, INSURABLE_ELEVATED_PREMIUM, INSURABLE_WITH_EXCLUSIONS, FURTHER_INVESTIGATION, UNINSURABLE.
Risk Tier
Qualitative summary of composite score: LOW (<30%), MEDIUM (30–60%), HIGH (60–80%), CRITICAL (>80% of maximum 125).
Replacement Cost
Estimated cost to procure, integrate, launch, and commission an equivalent replacement satellite at current market prices. Includes launch insurance allowance.
Depreciated Value
Replacement cost pro-rated by remaining design life fraction. Used as the insurable value basis for total loss calculations.
Revenue at Risk
Annualized revenue from satellite operations (transponder lease, data services, etc.) that would be lost in a total loss event. Source: operator disclosure or market rates.
Distribution Statement A
U.S. Government distribution statement indicating the document is approved for public release without restriction. Orbital Inspect applies this statement to UNCLASSIFIED reports for consistency with aerospace documentation standards.
C.2  Orbital Mechanics & Engineering Terms
GEO (Geostationary Earth Orbit)
Circular orbit at ~35,786 km altitude, 0° inclination. Satellite appears stationary relative to Earth's surface. Primary orbit for communications and weather satellites.
LEO (Low Earth Orbit)
Orbits between ~160 km and ~2,000 km altitude. Higher debris density and radiation from the South Atlantic Anomaly; shorter orbital periods (~90 min).
MEO (Medium Earth Orbit)
Orbits between ~2,000 km and ~35,786 km. Intersects the Van Allen radiation belts; used primarily for navigation constellations (GPS, Galileo, GLONASS).
MMOD (Micrometeoroid and Orbital Debris)
Collective term for natural micrometeoroids and man-made orbital debris particles. Primary cause of surface cratering, solar array cell damage, and thermal coating degradation.
TID (Total Ionizing Dose)
Cumulative radiation dose absorbed by electronic components over operational life. Measured in krad(Si). Exceeding design TID limit causes transistor parameter shifts and eventual component failure.
SEU (Single Event Upset)
A change in state of a memory cell or logic element caused by a single energetic particle strike. Can cause software errors, safe-mode entry, or in severe cases, latch-up requiring power cycle.
EOL (End of Life)
The point at which a satellite can no longer maintain its operational mission, typically defined by propellant depletion, power margin falling below minimum threshold, or critical component failure.
ESD (Electrostatic Discharge)
Sudden release of static charge built up on spacecraft surfaces due to the plasma environment. Can cause surface material damage, anomalous electrical events, and solar array degradation, particularly in GEO during geomagnetic storms.
Power Margin
Remaining available power as a percentage of beginning-of-life power generation capacity. A margin below 15% is considered critical. Primary driver of remaining life estimates.
Thermal Cycling
