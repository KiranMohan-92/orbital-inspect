## What It Really Is

- Best current description: a high-end “space asset first-look and underwriting copilot.”
- It is not yet an “orbital inspection operating system.”
- The good news is the repo already contains the right next-step primitives: evidence fusion, TLE history, conjunction risk, enhanced weather, reports, portfolio, and workflow scaffolding.

## How To Make It NASA/SpaceX-Class

- Move the primary path onto durable jobs, signed artifacts, immutable event logs, and explicit degraded-state semantics.
- Promote the existing evidence services into the main pipeline, then add telemetry, multi-epoch imagery, camera pose/calibration, CAD/digital-twin comparison, and deterministic CV.
- Separate “first-look triage” from “mission/claims decision.” The second path must be human-approved, source-traceable, and physics-checked.
- Add true tenant isolation, export-control-aware access, and review workflows before trying to sell into insurers, operators, or government.
- Reposition externally as `orbital infrastructure health OS`, not `Dyson swarm` yet.

## Dyson Swarm / Orbital Compute Growth

- The strongest future market is not generic satellite damage scoring. It is inspection, health scoring, and servicing intelligence for deployable power and compute infrastructure: solar arrays, radiators, beam directors, phased arrays, docking interfaces, robotic assembly nodes, and modular orbital data-center buses.
- `Musk/SpaceX-style` need, inferred: anomaly compression across massive fleets, pre/post-deployment QA, autonomous servicer tasking, and no-go decisions at scale.
- `Huang/NVIDIA-style` need, inferred: on-orbit edge AI that can fuse imagery/sensor streams locally, operate under radiation/thermal constraints, and support autonomous operations.
- `Pichai/Google-style` need, inferred: disconnected or air-gapped control planes, auditability, compliance, and latency-sensitive distributed compute.
- So the market ladder is: insurer/operator triage -> servicer/RPO intelligence -> orbital compute/power infrastructure certification.

As of April 1, 2026, the external market signals are real: NASA still frames in-space assembly/manufacturing as strategically important, even after canceling OSAM-1 for cost/schedule/partner reasons; Astroscale has real inspection heritage with ADRAS-J; OrbitsEdge is explicitly selling ruggedized orbital edge compute; Lonestar is selling cislunar/lunar data storage; NVIDIA and Google are both emphasizing edge, autonomous, latency-sensitive, and disconnected compute. I do not see official public evidence that Google or NVIDIA themselves are operating orbital data centers today; that part is inference from their platform direction and the emerging space-compute market.
